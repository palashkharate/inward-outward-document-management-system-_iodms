# Chapter 1 Introduction

## 1.1 Background

The aerospace industry demands high reliability, safety, and availability of aircraft systems. Modern aircraft increasingly employ digital avionics, intelligent diagnostics, and predictive maintenance to improve operational readiness while reducing maintenance costs. Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning (ML) have emerged as enabling technologies for analysing large volumes of operational data and estimating the Remaining Useful Life (RUL) of critical components. These technologies support condition-based maintenance and reduce unscheduled failures. **(Reference placeholder: [R1], [R2])**

## 1.3 Problem Motivation

Aircraft maintenance has traditionally relied on scheduled inspections. While effective, such maintenance may result in unnecessary component replacement or unexpected failures. Predictive maintenance addresses these limitations by estimating equipment health using historical sensor data.

In parallel, engineering organisations require secure, traceable document management systems. Existing manual or legacy solutions often lack maintainability, scalability, and modern software engineering practices. The IODMS project addresses these challenges for an air-gapped organisational environment.

## 1.4 Internship Objectives

1. Study avionics communication standards including ICD, MIL-STD-1553B and ARINC 429.
2. Understand industrial software engineering practices.
3. Analyse predictive maintenance techniques for aircraft engines.
4. Study and document the architecture and implementation of IODMS.
5. Develop professional technical documentation following engineering standards.
